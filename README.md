BIKESHARE PROJECT UDACITY

Overview: 

In this project,Python is used explore data related to bike share systems for three major cities in the United States—Chicago, New York City, and Washington.
Code is written to import the data and answer interesting questions about it by computing descriptive statistics. 
A  script is written to take in raw input to create an interactive experience in the terminal to present these statistics.

Statistics Computed: 

 In this project,the code  provides the following information:

#1 Popular times of travel (i.e., occurs most often in the start time)
Q1. What is the most popular month for bike traveling?
Q2. What is the most popular day of the week for bike rides?
Q3. What is the most popular hour of the day for bike rides?


#2 Popular stations and trip
Q5. What was the most popular trip from start to end?
Q6. What is the most popular start station?
Q7. What is the most popular end station?

#3 Trip duration
Q4. What was the total traveling done for 2017 through June, and what was the average time spent on each trip?


#4 User info
Q8. Types of users: subscribers, customers, others
Q9. What is the earliest, latest, and most frequent year of birth, respectively?
Q10. What is the breakdown of gender among users?
